Questions, answered straight

The things every buyer asks — with the same honesty as the rest of this page.

Will the AI ever email my contacts without my permission?
No. Every action the AI wants to take — a send, a score change, a tag — lands in an approval queue first, and outgoing emails require you to explicitly confirm. Nothing touches a contact without a human click. That's a design principle, not a setting.
How does it draft replies "in my voice"?
When you connect your mailbox, LeadSpot studies your own sent mail — how long your emails run, how you open and close, how formal you are — and builds a private style profile. Drafts are generated to match it. They're drafts, not sends: you always review before anything goes out.
What email providers do you support?
Gmail gets the full treatment today: triage into categories, voice-matched drafts, and automatic deal intelligence. Outlook currently gets deal intelligence from incoming mail, with full triage and drafting on the roadmap. If you're on something else, tell us — it shapes what we build next.
How is this different from a ChatGPT plugin or an AI sidebar?
Sidebars answer questions; they don't know your business. LeadSpot's assistant is wired into your actual pipeline and activity record, and it cites its evidence — ask "which deals are going cold?" and you get specific contacts with the receipts, not confident guesses. It also remembers the conversation, so follow-ups work like talking to a colleague.
What happens to my data? Is it used to train AI models?
Your CRM data stays yours and is used to serve you — triage, drafts, briefs. It is not used to train foundation models. Email content is processed to classify and draft, and we keep stored previews minimal rather than warehousing full mailboxes.
Can I use my own AI provider account?
Yes. Organizations can bring their own Anthropic API key, so AI usage runs on your account, under your terms, at your negotiated rates. If you don't have one, LeadSpot's built-in AI works out of the box.
How long does setup take?
Minutes, not weeks. Connect your mailbox, and LeadSpot backfills recent history — your inbox arrives already sorted, and the drafting engine learns your voice from mail you've already sent. There's no data-entry project and no mandatory training week, because filling itself in is the whole point.
We already have a CRM. How painful is switching?
Less than staying usually is. Contacts import in bulk, and because LeadSpot populates itself from live activity, you don't need to migrate years of stale notes to get value — the record rebuilds from what's actually happening. Most teams run both for a week and stop opening the old one.
What about unsubscribes and email compliance?
Handled by default. Every send — human or AI-initiated — is checked against your suppression list first, and compliance footers (unsubscribe link, physical address) are added automatically. Nobody on your team can accidentally email someone who opted out.
Who is LeadSpot for?
Small businesses, agencies, and lean sales teams — anyone where reps wear five hats and admin time is the difference between hitting the number and not. If you have a 40-person RevOps department tuning Salesforce, we're probably not your tool. If your CRM is currently "a spreadsheet and guilt," we very much are.

Early-access pricing is set with founding teams, and it's built to be a fraction of what the recovered admin time is worth — remember the math above: the product pays for itself if it saves each rep a couple of hours a week, and it saves more than that. Request access and we'll give you a number, not a "contact sales" runaround.
What doesn't it do yet?
Same honesty as above: scheduling an email for a future date isn't wired up yet, Outlook triage is still coming, and the AI won't run your whole pipeline unattended — by design, it proposes and you approve. We'd rather under-promise here and let the Tuesday above speak for itself.
